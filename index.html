What does it do?

Machine learning is a sub-field of artificial intelligence, which is defined as the ability of a machine to imitate human intelligence and behaviour. This model deals with the practical applications of logical and mathematical deductions. An extremely useful technology which allows machines to learn from data and self improve, in which the use of statistical methods and algorithms are trained in order to create classifications or predictions.

The are three primary functions of a machine learning system.

Descriptive: where the system uses the data provided in order to explain what has happened.

Predictive: where the system uses the data provided in order to predict what will happen.

Prescriptive: where the system uses the data provided in order to make suggestions for what action to take.

The methods in which machine learning works fall in to three main categories

Supervised machine learning

Supervised machine learning uses labelled datasets to train algorithms to assign a classification to data or to predict outcomes, which will learn and grow more accurate over time. An algorithm like this would be trained with pictures of dogs, apples, or anything else, all labelled by humans. The machine would be able to learn ways to identify and categorise these different images on its own.

Unsupervised machine learning

Unsupervised machine learning uses unlabelled data and looks for patterns or trends that may not be what are explicitly being looked for. This type of algorithm can look through online sales data and identify what different demographics have been purchasing and may give insight to future trends which can then be taken advantage of.

Reinforcement machine learning

Reinforcement machine learning is a technique that allows a software model to learn by trial and error in a more interactive environment to take the most appropriate action by establishing a reward system. Reinforcement learning can train models to drive autonomous cars or play games by telling the machine when it made a right decision, or by correcting an incorrect decision.

Machine learning is currently being used in many ways.

Recommendation algorithms are utilised by corporations such as Netflix and YouTube to make suggestions on what movies, TV shows or videos to watch based on your previous viewing history by analysing your preferences based on what you have watched or liked in the past and finding similar media that you are more likely to enjoy. These same algorithms are used by Amazon and other online retailers to provide suggestions on your next purchase based on what you have previously purchased or looked at.

Object detection and image analysis are used to analyse images for multiple points of information in order to do things like identifying different people in a picture, or being able to tell the difference between a cat and a dog. It is also used in the development of autonomous cars to identify its surroundings and acquire traffic data in order to make to the most logical and safest decisions possible

Speech and sound recognition are used to provide linguistic data for digital assistants such as Siri, Alexa, Cortana and Google Assistant. Every time a request is made to a digital assistant, the voice command is sent to the internet via the cloud, where it is reviewed and interpreted, then a relevant response is sent back to the device. If a response is not relevant then the user is able to correct it, and this new information is used in future requests, and as such continue to learn and develop more relevant responses.

Chatbots and helplines use machine learning algorithms for humans to speak with machines, instead of another human. These algorithms use natural language processing with bots to come up with relevant responses based on data collected from previous conversations.

Fraud detection can be applied by machine learning by analysing patterns, such as spending habits, how much is normally spend and where it’s being spent. If a transaction outside the normal acceptable parameters it can be flagged as a potentially fraudulent transaction. Similar processes can also be used to identify spam emails and fraudulent log-in attempts.

What does it do?

Raspberry Pi is non-profit, charity organisation which consider its purpose as helping to make electronics, software development and prototyping affordable and possible for wide masses of enthusiasts and makers, to attract school students to university degrees in Computer Science. It was reaction to decline the interest to IT in society after bursting IT bubble in the beginning of 2000’s. Founders of Raspberry Pi decided to provide a small, very inexpensive single board computer (SBC) for general purposes.

This idea has turned out to be very successful. A total of 30 million devices had been soldby December 2019 (see Illustration 1). Anumber of similar SBCs with open-source hardware architecture has emerged on the market after that. Among them Banana Pi, BeagleBoardcan be named.

The applications of Raspberry Pi boards have been extended far outside of educational area (the Rasbery Pi is used in almost a quarter of all UK secondary schools(NComputing, 2020)). At homes, it is used as a very affordable desktop PC for retro-gaming, network data storage and building cluster computer systems

For instance, Merton Council (UK) significantly reduced cost of its IT infrastructure by using small and cheap computers based on Raspberry Pi (NComputing, 2020).

The University of Cambridge, one ofthe world’s top research universities upgraded its IT. For that, the university has chosen the Raspberry Pi based Citrix Workspace as a desktop for 15,000 users; desktop PC consuming 450 W that’s powered 24/7 was replaced by Raspberry Pi which consumes between 5W and 15W, that brings a substantial energy saving (Citrix Systems, 2020).

Évreux Portes de Normandie (EPN) an inter-communal organisation, centred in the city of Évreux (France). It uses Stratodesk NoTouch Desktop, software which provides manages secure thin clients on numerous end-point Raspberry Pi computers (Stratodesk, 2019).

At the moment, Raspberry Pi provides a number of variations of Raspberry Pi, from smaller, low-cost SBC to more powerful SBC, capable to serve as an affordable, economical desktop PC. Other manufacturers provide a lot of various extension boards and accessories for Raspberry Pi. The last development are Raspberry Pi 400and Pico, they extend applications of Raspberry Pi even further.

Arduino is an open-source hardware and software company which designs and manufactures low-cost single board micro-controller kits. These boards can be programmed using C/C++ programming language and special Arduino Integrated Development Environment (IDE). The simplified API for access to the outside sensor and actuators, that does not need deep knowledge of micro-controller programming and makes it possible to use them by students and amateur hobbyists

Like Raspberry Pi, Arduino was organised at the beginning of 2000’s (in 2005 to be precise) with the prime purpose of using it in education. But Arduino boards have become very popular not only university students. Over the years, our products have been the brains behind thousands of projects, from everyday objects to complex scientific instruments.(Arduino, 2021) Special kits are developed for different levels of education including basic kits for middle school students, robotic arm and IoT kits for high school and university students

A lot of others produce, design and manufacture Arduino compatible boards and add-ons. Some Arduino boards are widely used by hobbyists and professionals to build IoT radio systems.

The company OpenEVSE started in February 2011 with a simple experiment to try to generate the SAE J1772 pilot signal on anArduino Development Board. One experiment led to another to another until a prototype J1772 compatible controller was born. With lots of feedback and interest from the great folks on the "My Nissan LEAF" Forum a few boards were offered to other hardware hackers. What started as a few boards (6 built in the first batch) turned into more and more... Today, OpenEVSE powers charging stations from many manufactures all over the world. (Open Evse, 2020)

The modern Arduino boards have capabilities to be programmed using high-level programming languages with support of Machine Learning. Also, Arduino boards are capable to do radio connections using various protocols.

What does it do?

Clouds and servers are a technology that is ever evolving. Cloud computing is the use of online servers, databases, software, and systems to provide computing services over the internet. The main difference, when comparing servers and cloud servers is that cloud servers are stored online over the internet. Whereas local servers are stored in physical locations on networks of Hard drives and other physical storage solutions.

With cloud technology the main benefits are speed, reliability, cost, and security.

Cost

When it comes to on site servers, there is a big demand for power electricity and cooling. All of which are not cost effective. There is also the need for more workers that are needed to establish data centres and help to run the software. The use of cloud technology means we can bypass most of these costs.

Reliability

Cloud technology allows users to store large amounts of data online to servers. And can be backed up to any location from anywhere in the world. Users do not need to spend money on hard drives when they are plenty of free cloud storage services provided. And these often have many options for different storage plans so its extremely flexible and can suit the users needs. It is also beneficial to businesses because of this reason and is much less expensive for them

Speed

Cloud services are available as self service and are available on demand most of the time. Substantial amounts of data can be compiled and stored in quick speeds. Internet technology has improved at an exponential level. In the current day data can be accessed almost instantaneously from around the world thanks to the internet and cloud technology.

Cloud computing is a quickly evolving technology. There are many aspects of cloud computing that are likely to develop in the next few years.

One such example is the amount of data we can store in the cloud. All forms of data are increasing in size very quickly. And thus, the demand for cloud data storage is also increasing at a quick rate. This demand has made data centres become more competitive and led them to offer more value and storage for much less cost.

Another future development is businesses going serverless. “The demand for serverless computing is expected to grow by 25% from 2021 and 2026.” (Shradda's, 2021). Going serverless means that businesses can develop and run applications without having the need to have physical servers. Applications will run on servers but there will no need for server maintenance on the businesses’ end. Instead, the cloud service provider does all the maintenance and upgrading.

AI in cloud computing is also one of the biggest trends now. For example, google search uses AI that lives in the cloud. AI and machine learning is a large part of why Google is so proficient in obtaining the search results for every user. AI and cloud together are one of the most quickly developing technologies. Google is not the only company that uses AI in this manner. Instagram, Facebook are just a few from the many that use such algorithms and AI to understand user’s data and improve their services.

What is the likely impact?

One of the impacts of cloud services is the cost savings. Especially with businesses. Once cloud systems are setup, the cost savings for companies are pretty good. Access to data is simple, fast, and cost effective. Also, most cloud offerings allow pay as you go system. So, there is no extra costs when there is an excess of storage capabilities when its not needed. This is highly effective as it allows organisations to only pay for what they need.

Another key factor when considering cloud systems is the environmental impact. Hosting servers on the cloud instead of locally means there is less of a carbon footprint. This is because there is less reliance on powering of physical servers. And thus, less use of electricity. “A Pike Research report predicted data centre energy consumption will drop by 31% from 2010 to 2020 based on the adoption of cloud computing and other virtual data options.” (SalesForce) .

Businesses are much more likely to be more secure when using cloud systems. The cloud software providers can more often bring effective updates to the software. Hence providing more reliable security for organisations. This however can be a negative because, usually when cloud systems are not used. There is an IT workforce that are involved in the maintenance of security, system updates, and infrastructure. The use of cloud means that these workers are not necessarily needed as much anymore, and thus decreasing employment for workers in this field.

However, there are still more benefits to the use of cloud by businesses. Cloud technology allows much more collaboration between different teams. Communication is much more easier using data from the cloud which is much more readily available. Workers from all different fields like engineers, digital marketing teams, UI designers and much more, can collaborate more efficiently.

What does it do?

Fully autonomous vehicles, such as cars and trucks are capable of operating without any human input. However partial automation has been part of many cars for some time. Automatic transmission and cruise control both aids, reduce the amount of human input needed to successfully drive. Advanced driver assistance systems have continued to be developed and deployed in modern cars including lane assistance to keep the driver on their part of the road, blind spot detection to avoid collisions, automatic parking and much more. All these technologies contribute to the trend towards fully driverless cars and the technological advances required for such systems.

A vehicles development can be defined within the 6 levels of autonomy system defined by the Society of Automotive Engineering (SAE). Level 0, no automation. Level 1, driver assistance. Level 2, partial automation. Level 3, conditional automation. Level 4, high automation. Level 5, full automation.

Leading cars available for consumers are usually around automation level 2 and 3. They operate with combinations of many technologies. These include GPS navigation, a network of cameras, lidar, radar and other sensors. Mercedes drive pilot system is one level 3 system. IT allows for the car to drive itself in highway conditions including high traffic or traffic jams. The driver must remain ready to take over if the system requests, however.

On the commercial side ‘Waymo’ operates a limited access self-driving taxi service aiming for level 4 or 5 automation functionality. Phoenix, Arizona provides dry sunny and flat conditions to simplify the conditions for the early stages of such a service. Variable conditions such as undulation, wet or lower visibility environments will continue to pose new challenges for self-driving AIs as usage expands.

Other kinds of vehicles, trucks and their specific issues – One company developing autonomous trucks ‘tu simple’ has had success as well. With drivers manually controlling the truck at each end of the delivery route, over 1500kms of highway was successfully navigated autonomously by a truck delivering watermelons.

The legality of autonomous vehicles is another obstacle in some locations. Current motor vehicle legislation in Australia uses terms such as ‘driver’ and ‘control’ who that would refer to when a driverless car breaks road rules or injures members of the community is unclear and rules must be drafted for such events. Additionally, privacy protection may also be an issue as location would be tracked and possibly accompanied by personal information, facial recognition (to verify ownership) or even vital sign information monitoring the condition of the driver. Who would have access to this information? What are they allowed to do with it? Will users who don’t wish to give said information be locked out of all services? All questions that must be addressed within a legal framework.

The security of autonomous vehicles in the event of a cyber attack is also a consideration especially when manual driver override is not a feasible option. If control of the car is not compromised, sensitive data as mentioned above could still be at risk.

A major technical limitation in the progress of self-driving autonomous vehicles is the lack of training data. Machine learning systems such as the AI that controls autonomous vehicles requires extraordinary amounts of data to perform well. The issue in this scenario is that this data is extremely expensive to collect. Other applications of machine learning are often digital and can run a simulation or game 1000s of times simultaneously to collect data. As companies continue to collect real world driving data as more cars capable to collect data hit the road and further simulate scenarios for the AI to learn from this should improve and continue to develop.

What is the likely Impact

As autonomous vehicles become both more accessible and more affordable there will be a range of societal impacts. As those currently unable to drive themselves for physical, psychological and other reasons will have flexibility and mobility not previously afforded to them. Elderly members of the community who have lost the right to drive, or ability to do so safely, will continue to be allowed to travel in their daily lives in the manner to which they are accustomed. Disabled individuals who are not able to drive will acquire a new level on independence as they will no longer rely on others from transportation. Driving will become more accessible and long distances more manageable for everyone. It should also be safer as drunk driving, inattentiveness and other road offences drastically reduce.

However, many people are currently employed in industries because of their ability to drive. Taxi’s, Ride Shares and perhaps even buses would all lose the need to employ an actual driver and still offer the same service. Tens of thousands are employed in this industry in Australia alone and would be without work.

The freight industry would also be affected, local deliveries could be replaced but the most significant change would likely be in long distance trucking. While truck drivers are restricted in the number of hours they are allowed to work within a given time to ensure the health and safety of the driver and other members of the community, such a regulation would likely not apply to autonomous vehicles. Being able to drive almost constantly is an economic and logistical prospect traditional drivers will simply be unable to compete with.

How will this affect me?

Currently I am able to drive myself but avoid doing so in various conditions including high traffic and avoid major urban centres. With a personal autonomous vehicle, I would be more comfortable travelling to many locations. It would also reduce the effort required to travel interstate to my hometown or visiting family members spread around the country. Of course, the price of such a vehicle would be out of my price range at the moment.

Driver fatigue is one issue that contributes to morbidity and mortality. Falling asleep and micro-sleeps have resulted in head on collisions. Country roads can be especially hazardous. One lady from my town was hit head on whilst travelling on a weekday afternoon on a seven kilometre straight and sustained major injuries. The other driver, also a local studying away was returning home and had fallen asleep at the wheel. Swerving to avoid animals on the road such as cattle, kangaroos or camels has resulted in rollovers. Some people overtake when they shouldn’t, risking head on collisions when medical assistance could be a long time arriving. People used to drive in city conditions often do not overtake safely.

My parents travel a lot on remote and outback roads as part of their work as remote teachers. OH&S policy and procedures remove some of the risk. Scheduled breaks, vehicle inspections and 4-wheel driver training help with driver safety, They have recently been issued with government vehicles, a Subaru and a Kia Sportage fitted with blind spot and rear cross traffic assist, lane follow assist and adaptive cruise control. They have commented positively on how this has been useful when driving long distances. The vehicles automatically reduce speed when approaching a vehicle travelling slower in front of them or alert them to lane deviation. In particular driving to Coober Pedy or Ceduna from Adelaide has been easier on fatigue.

project idea.

Overview

This cleaning and chore scheduling app will assist users in organising a functional cleaning and chore checklist, with assorted tasks that need to be done with varying frequencies in all areas of the home. This checklist can also be viewed by other members of the household, and each person can have specific tasks or locations assigned to them throughout any given time period in order to help share the load.

Motivation

As someone who works full time and now studies part time, it is difficult to set up a cleaning structure at home. It’s very easy to just get home and do nothing. Something like this sending reminders out to do specific things every now and then could hopefully be the little push needed to help motivate one to perform cleaning duties throughout the week.

Description

This app will have numerous features in order to help you to keep your house clean.While setting up this app, you will add in the number of rooms and separate areas in your house, with the ability to give each its own unique name. Additionally, multiple users can download the app and share information via the cloud. Any users can also be added to the same home and have tasks assigned to them. Notifications and reminders will be utilised on the day the task is due to be completed.

There will also be a list of cleaning tasks predefined such as vacuuming, dusting, clean windows, and so on. Each room will have its own list of tasks depending on the room itself, so your bedroom would have “change sheets, wipe down bedside table, clean windows” and depending on the contents of the bedroom, could also have “clean mirror, wipe down fan, etc”.

Each individual task will also have its own suggested frequency which can be altered at will. Changing sheets could be once a week, vacuuming three times a week, mowing the lawns once every two weeks, take the bins out every Wednesday and put on a load of laundry every two days.Users can be added and assigned tasks to be completed. So, John could vacuum “Johns room” on Sundays, Wednesdays and Fridays. An alternative assignment process would be by task. So once a week, Sally could clean all windows in the home. There will be multiple ways to view tasks to be done. By timespan will give an overview of everything to be done that day/week/month. By room will give an overview of each room/area and all tasks to be completed within. By user will give an overview of each user and all tasks assigned to them. Once completed tasks will be checked off until the next time the are due to be completed.
